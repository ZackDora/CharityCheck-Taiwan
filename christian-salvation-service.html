Committed to rescuing disadvantaged women, children and families in Taiwan

Christian Salvation Service
基督徒救世會

Introduction

Since its establishment in 1983, the Christian Salvation Society (CSS) has been committed to rescuing disadvantaged women, children and families in Taiwan, providing a variety of services, covering psychological counseling, legal consultation, childcare support and financial assistance.

So far, CSS has helped more than 2,000 families and individuals, assisting them to get through difficult times and rebuild a better life. In addition, CSS also actively participates in community activities and has launched a number of education and resource center programs to enhance the self-development capabilities of disadvantaged groups in society and promote social harmony and progress.

Types of Charity

  • Support Children and Families in Needs

    • Helps vulnerable children under 6-year-old with special needs to have proper medical care and support.

    Provides supports to families with low income status, women and families in special circumstances during times of crisis.

  • Post Adoption Service

    • Facilitates adoptive families and birth families to maintain contact, arrange roots visits and also facilitates reunions. (Also serves those birth families who require additional resources)

  • Parenting Support

    • Provides parenting tips and advice to assist parents in improving their parenting skills to have proper care for babies, and stable families.

    Provides parents and other caregivers who have attended CSS's parenting guidance program with short-term child care services that offer temporary relief, improve family stability, and reduce the risk of abuse or neglect.

  • Mental Health and Psychosocial Support

    • Helps parents facing challenges during the preschool years by offering counseling, workshops, and support groups. The aim is to prevent mental health risks, promote mental well-being, and improve parenting skills, foster better parent-child relationships and stronger spousal relationships to support effective child-rearing.

    Provides support to pregnant women and new mothers through depression screening, counseling sessions, and support groups. This early intervention helps manage prenatal and postpartum depression, improving the mother's mental health and her ability to care for her infant.

    Assists vulnerable families by facilitating access to newborn screenings for congenital illnesses, ensuring early diagnosis and intervention to reduce long-term health impacts, while also providing financial support when necessary.

  • Early Intervention for Vulnerable Children and Families

    • Supports vulnerable families with preschool children who may have developmental delays by helping them access needed resources for intervention or education and offering psychological support and counseling.

    Provides support to pregnant women and new mothers through depression screening, counseling sessions, and support groups. This early intervention helps manage prenatal and postpartum depression, improving the mother's mental health and her ability to care for her infant.

    Assists vulnerable families by facilitating access to newborn screenings for congenital illnesses, ensuring early diagnosis and intervention to reduce long-term health impacts, while also providing financial support when necessary.

  • Women's Support and Empowerment Center (Government commissioned programs)

    • The mission of the Support and Empowerment Center is to help disadvantaged women lead independent lives through support and empowerment. The center also aims to strengthen community connections and encourage participation.

    (centers: Taipei City Neihu Women's Support and Empowerment Center / Taipei City Songshan Nangang Women's Support and Empowerment Center)

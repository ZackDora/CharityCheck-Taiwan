Chung Foo Foundation
莊福文教基金會

Introduction

The Chuang Fu Cultural and Educational Foundation significantly impacts society through its diverse initiatives. It focuses on wild rhino conservation, engaging volunteers and promoting breeding efforts at Leofoo Village Theme Park. The foundation also protects native endangered species and enhances environmental education, exemplified by the Kinmen County Wildlife Conservation Research Base. Additionally, it supports underprivileged families by offering free park admission through the Dream Fulfillment Charity Day, benefiting over 70,000 participants since 2009, and hosts monthly charity days to further assist those in need.

Types of Charity

  • Improve Environmental Education

    • ● Plan animal conservation and environmental education courses, allowing people to participate in various ecological conservation promotion activities to learn correct knowledge about environmental ecology.

    ● In 2022, the Chuang Fu Foundation and the Kinmen County Government inaugurated the "Kinmen County Wildlife Conservation Research Base." Following the professional veterinary training courses, an environmental education guided tour was launched in November, open for all schools, families, and social welfare organizations to participate together in enjoyable activities.

  • Protection of Native endangered species

    • ● 2000- Donate Hodgson's hawk eagle documentary-《希望之徵》(Hodgson's hawk eagle Documentary Press Conference) for 14 years.

    ● 2009- Publish "Leofoo Village Station of Hsinchu County Wildlife Aid Center" assists in the rescue of wildlife in Hsinchu County.

  • Animals Conversation

    • Specialize in wild rhino conservation:

    ● 2015- Go to South Africa to learn about the conservation status of the rhino's natural habitat.

    ● 2016- Recruit rhino conservation volunteers to go to South Africa and participate in on-site conservation work, including the care of rhino orphans.

    ● 2021- The Chuang Fu Foundation Zoo (Leofoo Village Theme Park which was published in 1979) promotes rhinoceros breeding within the park.

    ● 2024- A healthy male rhino baby was born at the Leofoo Village Theme Park on July 13.

Donation Usages

  • Protect endangered species

    Donations will help the “Leofoo Village Station of Hsinchu County Wildlife Aid Center” enhance its capabilities in breeding and rehabilitating endangered wildlife, including rescue and medical care, breeding research, conservation promotion, and talent development.

  • Helping children

    Since 2009, the Dream Fulfillment Charity Day has been launched, inviting individuals from rural areas and underprivileged families to enter the park for free. By 2022, over 70,000 participants have benefited from this initiative over the course of 13 years.

    There is a charity day every month- On the 6th of each month, a group from designated organizations and those eligible for subsidies is invited.
